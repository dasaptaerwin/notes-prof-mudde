> The public must be able to understand the basics of science to make informed decisions. Perhaps the most dramatic example of the negative consequences of poor communication between scientists and the public is the issue of climate change, where a variety of factors, not the least of which is a breakdown in the transmission of fundamental climate data to the general public, has contributed to widespread mistrust and misunderstanding of scientists and their research ([Somerville and Hassol, 2011](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC3852879/#b18-june-12-e6)).
